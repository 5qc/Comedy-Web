What could the first month have in store for us?

January

January 1

  • At least 21 people are killed and displaced in Jakarta floods.

    • January 2

  • A missle strike issued by the United States hits near Baghdad International Airport in Iraq, which in result killed Iranian Major general Qasem Soleimani and Popular Mobilization Forces leader Abu Mahdi al-Muhandis.
  • Lebanese justice minister claims he was issued an arrest warrent, in which he fled his house arrest from Japan to Lebanon just days earlier.

    • January 3

  • In Kogi State, Nigeria, unidentified gunmen kill a total of 19. A church, school and several other buildings were ignited.
  • A man stabs three people in Villejuif, Île-de-France, killing one and injuring the rest. The attacker was later shot dead by police.
  • An airstrike crashes down onto a Popular Mobilization Forces convoy near Taji, destroying multiple vehicles, killing 6 and critically injuring 3 more, according to an Iraqi source.

    • January 4

  • 45th U.S. president Donald J. Trump targets 52 sites related or relating to Iranian culture if Iran strikes any Americans or American assets.
  • The Nicolás Maduro government announces that Venezuela will SELL some of it's oil and gold to fund it's cryptocurrency, petro.
  • Two people confirmed dead on Kangaroo Island, South Australia from ongoing bushfires.
  • Four people killed when a Mooney M20 plane crashes shortly after taking off near the island Lifou of New Caledonia.
  • A roadside bomb kills 14 people, including school children in Burkina Faso.

    • January 5

  • In the early hours of the morning, a drunk driver crashes into German tourists, killing 6 and injuring 11 more. The driver was later arrested and charged with murder.
  • 36 people are killed and many more injured after a building collapses during construction.
  • In Metz, Grand Est, France, an attacker attempted stabbing police officers while shouting "Allahu Akbar!"
  • Nearby bushfire leads the city of Eden, New South Wales to be evacuated.

    • January 6

  • A 5.8 magnitude earthquake strikes Puerto Rico causing landslides, power outages and slightly damaging homes.
  • American retailer company Pier 1 Imports closes 450 branches and might file for bankruptcy.
  • Police shoot dead a Turkish man that tried to attack officers in Germany.
  • Five soldiers are killed after their vehicle crashed into a roadside bomb.
  • A woman who was critically injured in the crash yesterday dies from her injuries, bringing the total death toll up to 7.
  • A court sentences Reynhard Sinaga to life imprisonment for 195 cases of rape, sexual assault, attempted rape and assault by penetration.
  • At least 30 people are killed in Northeastern Nigeria after an explosive device detonates. No group immediately took responsibility.

    • January 7

  • Four children are killed in an Al-Shabaab attack in Kenya.
  • A magnitude 6.4 earthquake strikes Puerto Rico, killing one and injuring several more.
  • CNN files a defamation lawsuit against Nicholas Sandmann for an undisclosed amount a year after the 2019 Lincoln Memorial confrontation.
  • An avalanche occurs in Kellogg, Idaho that killed 2 people and injures five more.

    • January 8

  • Iran begins attack the U.S. Starting with some missiles being fired at military bases and camps.
  • Al-Shabaab car bombing kills six people and injures 12 more in Mogadishu, Somalia.
  • A car bomb kills four Turkish soldiers when a vehicle explodes at the Syrian checkpoint.
  • A Boeing 737-800 was shot down by Iranian forces while taking off from Tehran Imam Khomeini International Airport, killing all 176 people onboard.
  • The trial of Satoshi Uematsu, a Japanese man who stabbed 19 disabled people, was held against trial and could be given a death penalty.

    • January 9

  • Islamist militants kill over 25 Nigerian soldiers in an attack on an army base. 63 more were also killed in a following shooutout.

    • January 10

  • A bombing that was claimed by ISIS takes place at a mosque in Quetta, Pakistan that killed 15 and injured 18.
  • Thousands and thousands of people protest in Melbourne, Brisbane and Sydney calling for Prime Minister Scott Morrison to resign, acucsing him of his negligence in the fires.
